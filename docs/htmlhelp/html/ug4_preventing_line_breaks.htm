Preventing line breaks

Sometimes you will want to keep two words together and ensure that they are never split by a line break. For example, a name such as RISC OS, or a telephone number, may not make as much sense to readers if it is broken up over two lines.

The way to do this is to enter a hard space between the words.

  • To enter a hard space, in RISC OS simply type Alt-space (ASCII character 160) where you would normally hit the space bar. In Windows, either type Alt-0160 (using the numeric keypad) or Right Alt-space.
  • A hard space is treated like an ordinary character and so is always the same width. If your text is fully justified, the space between the hard-spaced words will not be stretched to fill the line out but will remain the standard space.
  • Hard spaces are underlined; ordinary spaces are not.
