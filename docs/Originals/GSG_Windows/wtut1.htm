First steps in Fireworkz

About this chapter

This chapter will show you how to perform the most essential word processing tasks with Fireworkz. You’ll learn how to enter and correct text using the keyboard and how to use Fireworkz’ buttons, ruler and menus to format and manage your text.

Even if you are an experienced word processor and computer user, you should work through the exercises which follow to see the best way to perform these tasks in Fireworkz.

Topics covered include:

Loading a document

Getting ready to type by placing the caret within the document

Entering and correcting text

Moving text around the document using the cut, copy and paste buttons

Using the Fireworkz ruler to change margins and indent a paragraph

Using the justification buttons to position your text

Using the style button to create a heading

Printing your document

When you have completed this part of the tutorial you will be fully equipped to create and produce simple documents using Fireworkz, and ready to proceed to the next chapter.

Changing your view of the document

Changing the scale at which you view the document

If you have a low-resolution monitor, you won’t be able to see all of your document at once on screen. Only a portion of it will be visible in the window.

For some types of work, such as editing text, you might prefer a close-up view of part of the document. For others, such as checking that the whole document conforms to the style you have chosen and looks good, you’ll want to check the whole document at once, and a reduced size which enables you see it all at once on screen is appropriate.

If you have a higher resolution monitor, you can keep two or more views open simultaneously, one at 100% and another at a smaller size such as 50%, although this slows down the redrawing of the screen.

Changing the scale

To change the way the document is presented on screen, click on the View control button at the top left of the button bar:

The following dialogue box will appear:

Look at the group of buttons titled Scale. There are several choices available to you:

To see a scaled-down version of the document, click the button marked 50% and the OK button in the top right of the dialogue box.

To see the biggest version of the document that will fit on your screen, click the button marked Fit and then the OK button. This will shrink or expand the view so that you see one complete page across the screen.

Type in a percentage value, or use the arrows to increase or decrease the value shown in the box, then click OK.

You will probably find it easiest to edit text with the largest scale that shows you a full line of text on the screen.

Moving around the document

Changing the visible portion of the document

While you are typing, Fireworkz will always keep the caret on screen, so if you are simply typing continuous text you’ll only need to do this if you want to go and look at a part of the document which has scrolled off the screen.

Move the caret around

You can move the caret around the document in several ways:

Use the mouse to move the pointer around, and when you are pointing to the place where you want the caret to be, click the left mouse button. The caret will jump to the new position.

This is the best way to move around if you want to move the caret a long way.

Use the arrow keys on the keyboard to move the caret a line or a character at a time. The left and right arrow keys move the caret a character to the left and right respectively, and the up and down arrow keys move the caret a line up and down respectively.

Use the Page Up and Page Down keys to move through a document a screen at a time. The caret will automatically move up or down a screen when you press one of these keys.
Editing text

The simplest and most important word processing task is typing the text into the document.

Entering text

Adding text to a paragraph

To put new text in a particular part of a document, you first need to place the caret there.

Use the mouse to move the pointer to the start of the paragraph beginning ‘This year...’.

When you are pointing at the exact place you want to insert text, click the left button.

You’re ready to start typing. Enter the following text:

As you will realise, the annual school trip to the pantomime is almost upon us.

Only enter the words, punctuation marks and spaces and do not press Return at the end of lines; if a new line is needed Fireworkz will automatically begin one.

Type a space after the full stop, so that there is a single space between the full stop and the beginning of the next sentence.

Starting a new paragraph

To add a new paragraph you’ll need to start a new line.

Put the caret at the end of the paragraph by pointing there with the mouse and clicking the left button.

Press Return on the keyboard. The caret will move to the left margin ready for you to enter the following text:

If you have not already paid for your child’s ticket, please send £3 as soon as possible.

Adding dates

This letter needs to have today’s date added.

Put the caret at the end of the last line of the address and press Return to begin a new paragraph.

Display the Inserts menu from the Extra menu, and choose Date. The Insert date dialogue box appears:

Click on a date format, then click OK, to insert today’s date in that format in your document.

Today’s date is inserted in the document.

Moving text around the document

Some of the text in the letter is in the wrong order. It needs to be moved to the correct position by selecting, cutting and pasting it.

You use these buttons to perform these actions:

Selecting text to move

There are two ways to select areas of text. Try both and see which you prefer.

The first way is to:

Point at the beginning of the text you want to select (in this case the paragraph beginning ‘If you have...’.

Press and hold down the left mouse button while dragging the pointer to the left edge of the start of the next paragraph (but make sure that you don’t actually select any of the text in the next paragraph). Dragging as far as the start of the next paragraph ensures that you select the whole paragraph, including the paragraph return.

When you can see that the text you want to select is all highlighted, release the mouse button.

The text is selected.

The second way of selecting the text you need to move:

Place the caret at the beginning of the paragraph you just entered, ‘If you have...’.

Point to the end of the paragraph, just after the full stop, and press the Shift key while clicking the left mouse button.

As you do this you will notice that the text you are selecting is inverted so that it appears white on black. Also, the selection icon on the button bar turns solid black and many of the buttons which were dimmed are now illuminated.

Cutting and pasting

To move the selected text, follow these steps:

Click on the Cut button on the button bar. You will notice that a further button, the Paste button, is no longer dimmed.

Your selected text will disappear from the document, but it is stored internally by the computer until you paste it into a document, cut or copy another piece of text, or quit Fireworkz.

Put the caret at the beginning of the line ‘Yours faithfully’.

Click on the Paste button on the button bar.

The text will appear in the new position.

Correcting mistakes

Cutting enables you to remove text from a document but to keep a copy of it for use elsewhere. You can also do this by making a selection and then typing over it. However you will often want to remove text and throw it away, and you do this by using the backspace key. Typing over the text will cut it.

Scroll up to the top of the document. Look at the text in the first paragraph. You probably have already noticed that there are some mistakes and repeated words in it.

Deleting a single character

The word ‘Australians’ should not have the final ‘s’. To delete it:

Place the caret just after the word Australians, either with the mouse or using the arrow keys.

Press the backspace key. The unwanted character ‘s’ will be removed.

Deleting a word

In the first paragraph, the word ‘Cinderella’ is repeated by mistake. To correct this:

Place the caret anywhere within one of the occurrences of the word ‘Cinderella’, but when you do this double-click with the left mouse button.

The whole word will be selected and appear reversed out.

Press the backspace key as before.

The caret will now be in the space where the word was, with a space on either side of it. Press the backspace key again to remove the surplus space.

Deleting a block of text

Further down the paragraph the words ‘and retired footballers’ appear. To remove these:

Place the caret at the beginning of the space before the word ‘and’.

Select a block of text ending just before the full stop.

The selected text will appear reversed out (ie white on black) and the button bar will be illuminated, just as when you were cutting and pasting a block of text.

Press the backspace key. The words will disappear.

When you delete a block of text, you need to make sure that you don’t leave any double spaces between words.

Typing over a selection

If you simply want to replace text with different text:

Select the word(s) you want to replace. You can select anything from a single character to the complete contents of the document.

Type in the new word(s).

The old text will disappear and the new text will appear.

Formatting

The document you have been working on has a very simple format. Now it’s time to improve its look, by changing the justification, style, fonts and effects used in it.

Justification

Different parts of the document need to be aligned differently on the paper. The alignment of text in relation to the margins is called justification. For example, the address is usually flush against the right margin, headings are often centred so that they are in the middle of the paper, and the main body of text is often printed so that it is flush against both margins.

The letter you are working on requires each kind of justification. At the moment all the paragraphs are left justified. The lefthand edge of each line of text is next to the margin, but the righthand side ends where the last word finishes, producing a ragged right hand edge. Typesetters often refer to this as ‘Ragged right’. This is the default setting, which will appear in your documents if you don’t explicitly choose an alternative.

Choosing justification settings

Applying styles

One of the benefits of WYSIWYG word processing is that it enables you to experiment with styles and change the look of your document without having to print it out to see the results.

Changing the style of text is easily accomplished with Fireworkz’ buttons and menus.

Applying a style

The style of the main body of text in the letter you are editing looks fine and doesn’t need changing. However the heading could be made to stand out more.

To change the style of the heading:

First select the text you want to change, in this case the paragraph ‘VISIT TO PANTOMIME’.

Click on the Style button on the button bar. The Style dialogue box will appear:

Choose the style you want from the list, by scrolling through the list using the mouse until the entry you want is highlighted. In this case, choose the entry Heading.

Click Apply in the dialogue box.

The selected text will now appear in the new style.

Applying effects

Emphasis is added to text using bold and italic forms of the letters. If you have used a typewriter, you will be used to underlining words for emphasis, or to show that they are the title of a work of art. You should use italic letters to do this with Fireworkz.

To put the name of the pantomime in italics:

Double-click on the word Cinderella in the first paragraph, so that it is selected.

Click on the Italic button in the button bar.

The word will appear in italics.
Checking your work

Everybody makes typing errors from time to time. To help you avoid making mistakes, Fireworkz includes a spelling checker which will read your document and query any words it thinks you might have got wrong.

To check the spelling in this document:

Choose Spell from the Extra menu, and click on Check.

The spelling checker will pick up some words it does not know in the address. Bypass these by clicking on Next until you come to the first real mistake, ‘atending’.

The spelling checker will pick up the first spelling mistake and display a dialogue box showing the word ‘atending’.

Click on the correct spelling, attending, in the list box, and click Replace.

If there are any more spelling mistakes, correct them as the spelling checker reports them to you.

Click on the Print button in the document window.

You want to print one copy of the document, so there is no need to alter any of the settings in the dialogue box.

Click OK and the document should be printed. If there are any problems with printing, refer to the chapter on Printing in the User Guide.
